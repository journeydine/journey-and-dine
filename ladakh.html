Our Recommendation

Which Bike to Choose

We Rode This ✓
Himalayan 411
RE Himalayan · 411cc · Included in package
  • Included in the base package at no extra cost
  • Lighter — easier to pick up if it tips over on gravel
  • Handles all of Ladakh's passes and roads without issue
  • Proven machine — thousands of riders have done this exact route on it
  • A first-timer's best starting point for Himalayan riding
Himalayan 450
RE Himalayan · 450cc · Add-on · ₹2,000/day
  • More power at altitude — noticeably smoother above 15,000 ft
  • Better power delivery on steep, rocky passes
  • More confidence on the Shyok offroad and Wari La sections
  • Worth considering if you've already done the 411 on Ladakh before
Our honest take: We did this entire 2000 km route on the 411 — and it handled everything Ladakh threw at it. Khardung La, Tanglang La, the Shyok offroad to Pangong — the 411 never let us down. Don't let anyone pressure you into upgrading before your first ride. Do it on the 411, experience the route, and if you come back for a second time, step up to the 450.

Day by Day

The Full 11-Day Journey

Day1
✈️ Arrival in Srinagar
5,200 ft
Acclimatize · Briefing · Rest
  • Land at Srinagar Airport, transfer to pre-booked hotel
  • Evening briefing from road captain — route, rules, safety
  • Relax, have a good dinner, sleep early
Don't underestimate Day 1. Good sleep tonight sets the tone for everything. Skip the Dal Lake shikara — you have bigger days ahead.
Day2
🏍️ Srinagar → Kargil
220 km · 6-7 hrs
Zojila Pass · Drass · Tiger Hills · 8,780 ft
  • First full day on the bike — engines roar out of Srinagar after breakfast
  • Cross Zojila Pass (11,575 ft) — first high-altitude taste of the trip
  • Pass through Drass, the second coldest inhabited place on Earth
  • Visit Tiger Hills — iconic Kargil War vantage point
  • Reach Kargil by evening, deposit bike keys with captain
Zojila can be slippery even in summer. Follow the road captain's pace. Don't rush this pass — it rewards patience, not speed.
Day3
🏍️ Kargil → Leh
240 km · 6-7 hrs
Dha-Hanu · Magnetic Hill · Gurudwara · 11,600 ft
  • Stop at Dha-Hanu village — home to the last Indo-Aryan community in India
  • Confluence of Zanskar and Indus Rivers — unmissable photo stop
  • Visit Magnetic Hill where your bike appears to roll uphill
  • Brief stop at Gurudwara Pather Sahib and Hall of Fame
  • Arrive Leh, check in and rest — acclimatization is critical here
Altitude hits differently in Leh. Don't push yourself. Drink water constantly, avoid alcohol tonight, and walk slowly. AMS (altitude sickness) is real at 11,600 ft.
Day4
🏍️ Leh → Nubra Valley
160 km · 5-6 hrs
Khardung La · Cold Desert · 10,000 ft
  • Cross Khardung La — one of the world's highest motorable passes at 18,380 ft
  • The descent into Nubra is one of the most dramatic on the entire route
  • Arrive in Hunder — the cold desert of Nubra Valley
  • Optional camel ride on Bactrian double-humped camels (additional cost)
  • Swiss camp stay overnight in Nubra
Khardung La is the emotional peak of the trip. The air is thin and the views are otherworldly. Stop. Breathe it in. Take your time at the top — this is what you came for.
Day5
🏍️ Nubra → Turtuk → Nubra
196 km · 8-9 hrs
Turtuk Village · Shyok River · LOC · 9,850 ft
  • Ride to Turtuk — India's last village before the Line of Control, formerly under Pakistan until 1971
  • The Shyok River Valley along this route is jaw-dropping
  • Explore Turtuk's unique Balti culture — apricot orchards, stone houses, warm people
  • Return to Nubra Valley camp for the night
Turtuk is one of the most underrated stops on this entire trip. The people here are extraordinarily warm and the landscape is unlike anything else in India. Don't rush it.
Day6
🏍️ Nubra → Pangong Tso
160 km · 7-8 hrs
Shyok Route · Blue Lake · 14,270 ft
  • Off-road route via Shyok River or Wari La Pass — this is where the real adventure begins
  • Army road, river crossings, loose gravel — the toughest riding of the trip
  • First sight of Pangong Tso — the lake that shifts between five shades of blue in a single hour
  • Overnight in a camp right on the lakeshore
Nothing prepares you for Pangong. You'll round a corner and the lake will just be there — impossibly blue at 14,000 ft. It genuinely feels like another planet. Camp here and watch the sunset.
Day7
🏍️ Pangong → Leh via Chang La
160 km · 5-6 hrs
Chang La · 3 Idiots School · Thiksey · 11,600 ft
  • Cross Chang La — third highest motorable pass in the world at 17,590 ft
  • Stop at Druk White Lotus School — the real school from the movie 3 Idiots
  • Visit Thiksey Monastery if time permits — stunning 12-storey gompa overlooking the valley
  • Return to Leh for the night — rest day deserved
The 3 Idiots school stop always gets a reaction from the group. It's smaller than the movie makes it look, but still a cool moment on the route.
Day8
🏍️ Leh → Sarchu
250 km · 6-7 hrs
Nakee La · Lachung La · Tanglang La · 14,100 ft
  • Longest day of the trip — cross three high passes back to back
  • Tanglang La at 17,582 ft — your last major Himalayan pass
  • Saying goodbye to Ladakh as the landscape shifts from brown rock to green Himachal
  • Arrive Sarchu — Swiss camp stay at 14,100 ft on the Manali-Leh Highway
Three passes in one day is the most physically demanding stretch of the trip. Start early, pace yourself, and don't stop too long at altitude. The descent into Sarchu feels like the world opening up after hours of barren rock.
Day9
🏍️ Sarchu → Manali
200 km · 5-6 hrs
Final leg · Kullu Valley · 6,700 ft
  • Early start — descend through Rohtang Pass into green Himachal Pradesh
  • The contrast after 8 days of barren Himalayan terrain is surreal — suddenly trees
  • Arrive Manali — deposit bike keys, celebrate the completion of the ride
  • Overnight stay in Manali — well-earned dinner with the group
This day has a bittersweet feeling. The ride is done, the bikes go back, and you're surrounded by pine trees again. Most of our group sat in silence for a while. It just hits different.
Day10
🚌 Manali → Delhi (Volvo)
540 km · Overnight
Checkout · Explore Manali · Evening Departure
  • Checkout at 10 AM, explore Manali cafes and market with fellow riders
  • Relive the journey over chai and Manali's famous side streets
  • Evening departure on luxury semi-sleeper Volvo to Delhi
  • Overnight journey — breakfast on the bus
Day11
🏁 Arrival in Delhi
Trip Complete
2,000 km · 5 passes · 1 frozen lake · Memories for life
  • Arrive Delhi early morning
  • Trip ends — online completion certificate issued
  • The Ladakh ride is now something you've actually done
